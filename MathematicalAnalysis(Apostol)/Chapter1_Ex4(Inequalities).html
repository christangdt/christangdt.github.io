            Ex 1.23

            Prove Lagrange's identity for real numbers:

            (∑k=1nakbk)2=(∑k=1nak2)(∑k=1nbk2)−∑1≤k<j≤n(akbj−ajbk)2.

            Note that this identity implies the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality.

            Proof

            Use induction. For n=1, we have (a1b1)2=a12b12 and the identity is valid. Suppose the identity is valid for n=m−1, then

            (∑k=1makbk)2=(∑k=1m−1akbk+(ambm))2=(∑k=1m−1akbk)2+am2bm2+2ambm∑k=1m−1akbk=(∑k=1m−1ak2)(∑k=1m−1bk2)+am2bm2+2ambm∑k=1m−1akbk−∑1≤k<j≤m−1(akbj−ajbk)2

            Denote Am=∑k=1mak2 and Bm=∑k=1mbk2, then

            AmBm=(Am−1+am2)(Bm−1+bm2)=Am−1Bm−1+Am−1bm2+Bm−1am2+am2bm2

            thus

            Am−1Bm−1+am2bm2+2ambm∑k=1m−1akbk=AmBm−Am−1bm2−Bm−1am2+2ambm∑k=1m−1akbk=AmBm−∑k=1m−1(akbm−ambk)2

            which means

            (∑k=1makbk)2=AmBm−∑k=1m−1(akbm−ambk)2−∑1≤k<j≤m−1(akbj−ajbk)2=AmBm−∑1≤k<j≤m(akbj−ajbk)2

            thus the identity is valid for n=m.

            Ex 1.24

            Prove that for arbitrary real ak,bk,ck we have

            (∑k=1nakbkck)4≤(∑k=1nak4)(∑k=1nbk2)2(∑k=1nck4).

            Proof

            use Theorem 1.23 (Cauchy-Schwarz inequality) twice, we have

            (∑kakbkck)4=((∑kakbkck)2)2≤(∑k(akck)2∑kbk2)2=(∑k(ak2)(ck)2)2(∑kbk2)2≤(∑kak4∑kck4)(∑kbk2)2

            Ex 1.25

            Prove Minkowski's inequality:

            (∑k=1n(ak+bk)2)1/2≤(∑k=1nak2)1/2+(∑k=1nbk2)1/2.

            This is the triangle inequality ∥a+b∥≤∥a∥+∥b∥ for n-dimensional vectors.

            Proof

            From Cauchy-Schwarz inequality we have

            2∑k(akbk)≤2(∑kak2)1/2(∑kbk2)1/2

            thus

            ∑k(ak+bk)2=∑kak2+∑kbk2+2∑k(akbk)≤∑kak2+∑kbk2+2(∑kak2)1/2(∑kbk2)1/2=((∑kak2)1/2+(∑kbk2)1/2)2

            the inequality follows by taking square roots at both sides.

            Ex 1.26

            If a1≥a2≥⋯≥an and b1≥b2≥⋯≥bn, prove that

            (∑k=1nak)(∑k=1nbk)≤n∑k=1nakbk.

            Proof

            Use the notation [(Aij)] to define the expression which add all element of the matrix Aij. From the condition given, we have ∑1≤j≤k≤n(ak−aj)(bk−bj)≥0, which means

            (1)∑1≤j≤k≤n(akbk+ajbj)≥∑1≤j≤k≤n(akbj+ajbk)

            Next some simple calculation shows

            (∑k=1nak)(∑k=1nbk)=[(a1b1⋯a1bn⋮⋮anb1⋯anbn)]

            and still simple calculation shows

            ∑1≤j≤k≤nakbj=[(a1b1⋮⋱anb1⋯anbn)],∑1≤j≤k≤najbk=[(a1b1⋯a1bn⋱⋮anbn)]

            thus

            (2)∑1≤j≤k≤n(akbj+ajbk)=[(a1b1⋯a1bn⋮⋮anb1⋯anbn)]+∑k=1nakbk=(∑k=1nak)(∑k=1nbk)+∑k=1nakbk

            Also, we have

            (3)∑1≤j≤k≤n(akbk+ajbj)=[(a1b1+a1b1a1b1+a2b2⋯a1b1+anbna2b2+a2b2⋯a2b2+anbn⋱anbn+anbn)]=(n+1)∑k=1nakbk

            Combine expression 1,2,3, we get the result.

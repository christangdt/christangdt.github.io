          Absolutely continuous functions

          A real-valued function f defined on [a,b] is said to be absolutely continuous on [a,b] if for every ε>0 there is a δ>0 such that

          ∑k=1n|f(bk)−f(ak)|<ε

          for every n disjoint open subintervals (ak,bk) of [a,b], n=1,2,…, the sum of whose length ∑k=1n(bk−ak) is less than δ.

          Absolutely continuous functions occur in the Lebesgue theory of integration and differentiation. The following exercises give some of their elementary properties.

          6.11

          Prove that every absolutely continuous function on [a,b] is continuous and of bounded variation on [a,b].

          Proof

          Let f be an absolutely continuous function on [a,b]. For every ε>0, there is a δ>0 which satisfy the conditions of absolutely continuous, then whenever x,y∈[a,b] with |x−y|<δ, we let a1=min(x,y) and b1=max(x,y), and will have ∑k=11(b1−a1)<δ, thus ∑k=11|f(b1)−f(a1)|=|f(x)−f(y)|<ε, so f is uniformly continuous on [a,b], thus continuous on [a,b].

          Let δ1 be the number which satisfy the conditions of absolutely continuous for ε=1, since δ>0, we can find a number N such that a+Nδ1≤b and a+(N+1/2)δ1>b. Let P1={a,a+δ1/2,…,a+Nδ1,b}, for any P∈P[a,b], consider P∪P1, suppose there are points x1,…,xn∈P such that a<xi<a+δ1/2 for i=1,…,n, let a=x0,a+δ1=xn+1, then the subintervals (xi,xi+1) with i=0,…,n satisfy ∑i=0n(xi+1−xi)=δ1/2<δ1, so ∑i=0n|f(xi+1)−f(xi)|<1. We can prove a similar result for points of P∪P1 which lie in the interval [a+kδ1/2,a+(k+1)δ1/2] for k=1,…,2N, so we have

          ∑(P)≤∑(P∪P1)≤(2N+1)⟹Vf(a,b)≤2N+1

          this shows f is of bounded variation.

          6.12

          Prove that f is absolutely continuous if it satisfies a uniform Lipschitz condition of order 1 on [a,b].

          Proof

          Let ε>0, for every n disjoint open subintervals (ak,bk) of [a,b], n=1,2,…, we have

          |f(bk)−f(ak)|<M|bk−ak|

          for some M>0. So let δ=ε/M, if ∑k=1n(bk−ak)<δ, we have

          ∑k=1n|f(bk)−f(ak)|<∑k=1nM|bk−ak|=M∑k=1n(bk−ak)<Mδ=ε

          6.13

          If f and g are absolutely continuous on [a,b], prove that each of the following is also: |f|, cf (c constant), f+g, f⋅g; also f/g if g is bounded away from zero.

          Proof

          For |f|, we can prove from ||f(bk)|−|f(ak)||≤|f(bk)−f(ak)|.

          For cf, we choose δ such that ∑k=1n|f(bk)−f(ak)|<ε/|c| if c≠0, and the case c=0 is obviously true.

          For f+g, choose δ such that

          ∑k=1n|f(bk)−f(ak)|<ε2,∑k=1n|g(bk)−g(ak)|<ε2

          For fg, since f and g are continuous on [a,b], they are bounded, choose M such that |f|<M and |g|<M on [a,b], and choose δ such that

          ∑k=1n|f(bk)−f(ak)|<ε2M,∑k=1n|g(bk)−g(ak)|<ε2M

          For f/g, we can find m such that |g|≥m>0 on [a,b], choose δ such that

          ∑k=1n|g(bk)−g(ak)|<εM2

          then 1/g is absolutely continuous on [a,b], and then use f/g=f⋅(1/g).

        Existence theorems for integral and differential equations

        The following exercises illustrate how the fixed-point theorem for contractions (Theorem 4.48) is used to prove existence theorems for solutions of certain integral and differential equations. We denote by C[a,b] the metric space of all real continuous functions on [a,b] with the metric

        d(f,g)=∥f−g∥=maxa≤x≤b|f(x)−g(x)|

        and recall that C[a,b] is a complete metric space. (Exercise 4.67).

        7.36

        Given a function g∈C[a,b], and a function K continuous on the rectangle Q=[a,b]×[a,b], consider the function T defined on C[a,b] by the equation

        T(φ)(x)=g(x)+λ∫abK(x,t)φ(t)dt,λ∈R

        a) Prove that T maps C[a,b] into itself.

        b) If |K(x,y)|≤M on Q, where M>0, and if |λ|<M−1(b−a)−1, prove that T is a contraction of C[a,b] and hence has a fixed point φ which is a solution of the integral equation φ(x)=g(x)+λ∫abK(x,t)φ(t)dt.

        Proof

        (a) Let φ∈C[a,b], then to prove T(φ)∈C[a,b] we only need to show ∫abK(x,t)φ(t)dt is continuous on [a,b]. That φ∈C[a,b] means φ is bounded and we can find G>0 such that |φ|≤G on [a,b]. That K continuous on Q means for ∀ε>0 there exists δ>0 such that |(x1,y1)−(x2,y2)|<δ means |K(x1,y1)−K(x2,y2)|<ε/(Gb−Ga), so when |x−x′|<δ we have

        |∫abK(x,t)φ(t)dt−∫abK(x′,t)φ(t)dt|=|∫ab[K(x,t)−K(x′,t)]φ(t)dt|≤∫abεG(b−a)Gdt=ε

        this means ∫abK(x,t)φ(t)dt is continuous on [a,b].

        (b) Let φ,ϕ∈C[a,b], then

        ∥T(φ)−T(ϕ)∥=‖λ∫abK(x,t)[φ(t)−ϕ(t)]dt‖=maxa≤x≤b|λ|M|∫ab[φ(t)−ϕ(t)]dt|≤|λ|M∫ab∥φ−ϕ∥dt=|λ|M(b−a)∥φ−ϕ∥<∥φ−ϕ∥

        this means T is a contraction of C[a,b].

        7.37

        Assume f is continuous on a rectangle Q=[a−h,a+h]×[b−k,b+k], where h>0,k>0.

        a) Let φ be a function, continuous on [a−h,a+h], such that (x,φ(x))∈Q for all x∈[a−h,a+h]. If 0<c≤h, prove that φ satisfies the differential equation y′=f(x,y) on (a−c,a+c) and the initial condition φ(a)=b if and only if φ satisfies the integral equation

        φ(x)=b+∫axf(t,φ(t))dton (a−c,a+c)

        b) Assume that |f(x,y)|≤M on Q where M>0, and let c=min{h,k/M}. Let S denote the metric subspace of C[a−c,a+c] consisting of all φ such that |φ(x)−b|≤Mc on [a−c,a+c]. Prove that S is a closed subspace of C[a−c,a+c] and hence that S is itself a complete metric space.

        c) Prove that the function T defined on S by the equation

        T(φ)(x)=b+∫axf(t,φ(t))dt

        maps S into itself.

        d) Now assume that f satisfies a Lipschitz condition of the form

        |f(x,y)−f(x,z)|≤A|y−z|

        for every pair of points (x,y) and (x,z) in Q, where A>0. Prove that T is a contraction of S if h<1/A. Deduce that for h<1/A the differential equation y′=f(x,y) has exactly one solution y=φ(x) on (a−c,a+c) such that φ(a)=b.

        Proof

        (a) If φ satisfy φ′=f(x,φ(x)) and the initial condition φ(a)=b, then we have

        ∫axφ′(t)dt=∫axf(t,φ(t))dt=φ(x)−φ(a)⟹φ(x)=b+∫axf(t,φ(t))dt

        Conversely, if φ(x)=b+∫axf(t,φ(t))dt on (a−c,a+c), then φ(a)=b+∫aaf(t,φ(t))dt=b and φ′(x)=f(x,φ(x)), thus it satisfy the given differential equation and the initial condition.

        (b) Let ϕ∈S′, then for ε>0 there is φ∈S such that ∥φ−ϕ∥<ε, thus

        |ϕ(x)−b|≤|ϕ(x)−φ(x)|+|φ(x)−b|<∥ϕ−φ∥+|φ(x)−b|<Mc+ε

        thus we get |ϕ(x)−b|≤Mc and ϕ∈S.

        (c) If φ∈S, then

        |T(φ)(x)−b|=|∫axf(t,φ(t))dt|≤M|∫axdt|=M|x−a|≤Mc,x∈[c−a,c+a]

        thus we have T(φ)∈S.

        (d) If h<1/A, then

        ∥T(φ)−T(ϕ)∥=maxx∈[a−h,a+h]|∫ax[f(t,φ(t))−f(t,ϕ(t))]dt|≤maxx∈[a−h,a+h]∫ax|f(t,φ(t))−f(t,ϕ(t))|dt≤maxx∈[a−h,a+h]∫axA|φ(t)−ϕ(t)|dt≤Ah∥φ−ϕ∥<∥φ−ϕ∥

        so T is a contraction of S, thus there is a unique φ∈S such that φ(x)=b+∫axf(t,φ(t))dt, the result follows from (a).

          6.8

          Let f and g be complex-valued functions defined as follows:

          f(t)=e2πitif t∈[0,1],g(t)=e2πitif t∈[0,2]

          a) Prove that f and g have the same graph but are not equivalent according to the definition in Section 6.12.

          b) Prove that the length of g is twice that of f.

          Proof

          (a) Let Sf be the graph of f and Sg be the graph of g, since f(t)=g(t) for t∈[0,1], we see Sf⊆Sg, also g(t−1)=f(t) for t∈[1,2], thus Sg⊆Sf and so Sg=Sf. To see they are not equivalent, assume u:[0,2]→[0,1] is continuous and strictly monotonic and g=f∘u on [0,2], then g(t)=f(u(t)) means e2πit=e2πiu(t) for any t∈[0,2], so

          e2πiu(0)=e2πiu(1)=e2πiu(2)=1

          with u(0),u(1),u(2)∈[0,1] and either u(0)<u(1)<u(2) or u(0)>u(1)>u(2), but if t∈[0,1], the only possible values to make e2πit=1 are t=0 or t=1, so we get a contradiction. Thus no such u can exist.

           

          (b) Let P={t0,t1,…,tm}∈P[0,1], let tm+i=1+ti for i=1,…,m, then the partition P′:={t0,t1,…,t2m}∈P[0,2], and it is easy to see

          2Λf(P)=2∑k=1n|f(tk)−f(tk−1)|=∑k=1n|g(tk)−g(tk−1)|+∑k=1n|g(tm+k)−g(tm+k−1)|=∑k=12n|g(tk)−g(tk−1)|=Λg(P′)

          Thus we have 2Λf(P)≤Λg(0,2) for all P and 2Λf(0,1)≤Λg(0,2). Conversely, let P={t0,t1,…,tm}∈P[0,2], add 1 if the point 1 is not in P, then P∪{1} can be split into two partitions P1∈P[0,1] and P2∈P[1,2], let P2′={x−1:x∈P2}, then P2′∈P[0,1], and

          Λg(P)≤Λg(P∪{1})=Λg(P1)+Λg(P2)=Λf(P1)+Λf(P2′)≤2Λf(0,1)

          so we have Λg(0,2)≤2Λf(0,1). In conclusion we get Λg(0,2)=2Λf(0,1).

          6.9

          Let f be a rectifiable path of length L defined on [a,b], and assume that f is not constant on any subinterval of [a,b]. Let s denote the arc-length function given by s(x)=Λf(a,x) if a<x≤b, s(a)=0.

          a) Prove that s−1 exists and is continuous on [0,L].

          b) Define g(t)=f[s−1(t)] if t∈[0,L] and show that g is equivalent to f. Since f(t)=g[s(t)], the function g is said to provide a representation of the graph of f with arc length as parameter.

          Solution

          (a) Theorem 6.19 shows that s is strictly monotonic and continuous on [a,b], which is a compact interval, use Theorem 4.29 we see s−1 exists and is continuous.

           

          (b) From g(t)=f[s−1(t)] we see that

          g(s(t))=f[s−1(s(t))]=f(t),t∈[0,L]

          so s:[a,b]→[0,L] is a continuous and strictly monotonic function which makes f=g∘s, and the equivalence relation holds.

          6.10

          Let f and g be two real-valued continuous functions of bounded variation defined on [a,b], with 0<f(x)<g(x) for each x∈(a,b), f(a)=g(a),f(b)=g(b). Let h be the complex-valued function defined on the interval [a,2b−a] as follows:

          h(t)=t+if(t),if a≤t≤b,h(t)=2b−t+ig(2b−t),if b≤t≤2b−a.

          a) Show that h describes a rectifiable curve Γ.

          b) Explain, by means of a sketch, the geometric relationship between f, g, and h.

          c) Show that the set of points

          S={(x,y):a≤x≤b,f(x)≤y≤g(x)}

          is a region in R2 whose boundary is the curve Γ.

          d) Let H be the complex-valued function defined on [a,2b−a] as follows:

          H(t)=t−12i[g(t)−f(t)],if a≤t≤b,H(t)=t+12i[g(2b−t)−f(2b−t)],if b≤t≤2b−a.

          Show that H describes a rectifiable curve Γ0 which is the boundary of the region

          S0={(x,y):a≤x≤b,f(x)−g(x)≤2y≤g(x)−f(x)}

          e) Show that S0 has the x-axis as a line of symmetry. (The region S0 is called the symmetrization of S with respect to the x -axis.)

          f) Show that the length of Γ0 does not exceed the length of Γ.

          Solution

          (a) Let P∈P[a,2b−a], then P∪{b}=P1∪P2 where P1∈P[a,b] and P2∈P[b,2b−a], denote P1={t0,t1,…,tm} and P2={s0,s1,…,sn}, then

          Λh(P)≤Λh(P1)+Λh(P2)=∑k=1m∥h(tk)−h(tk−1)∥+∑j=1n∥h(sj)−h(sj−1)∥

          and we have

          ∑k=1m∥h(tk)−h(tk−1)∥≤∑k=1m(|tk−tk−1|+|f(tk)−f(tk−1)|)≤(b−a)+Vf(a,b)∑j=1n∥h(sj)−h(sj−1)∥≤∑j=1n(|tk−tk−1|+|g(2b−tk)−g(2b−tk−1)|)≤(b−a)+Vg(a,b)

          so Λh(P)≤2(b−a)+Vf(a,b)+Vg(a,b), since f and g are of bounded variation, we see that Λh(P) is bounded above for any P∈P[a,2b−a].

           

          (b) Geometrically, if we draw the graph of f and g in R2, since f(a)=g(a) and f(b)=g(b), their graphs form a closed region, which coincide with the graph of h in the complex plane.

           

          (c) From (b) we can see this is true.

           

          (d)-(f) The definition of H(t) seems to be wrong, since the graph of H cannot form a closed region.

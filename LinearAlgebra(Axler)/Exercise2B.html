                    3

                    (a) Let U be the subspace of R5 defined by

                    U={(x1,x2,x3,x4,x5)∈R5:x1=3x2 and x3=7x4}.

                    find a basis of U.

                    (b) Extend the basis in part (a) to a basis of R5.

                    (c) Find a subspace W of R5 such that R5=U⊕W.

                    Solution:

                    (a) One basis of U can be

                    (1,3,0,0,0),(0,0,7,1,0),(0,0,0,0,1)

                    (b) Supplement (a) with v1=(0,1,0,0,0),v2=(0,0,1,0,0).

                    (c) We have W={(0,0,a,b,0):a,b∈R}.

                    4

                    (a) Let U be the subspace of C5 defined by

                    U={(z1,z2,z3,z4,z5)∈C5:6z1=z2 and z3+2z4+3z5=0}.

                    (b) Extend the basis in part (a) to a basis of C5.

                    (c) Find a subspace W of C5 such that C5=U⊕W.

                    (6,1,0,0,0),(0,0,3,0,−1),(0,0,0,3,−2)

                    (b) Supplement (a) with v1=(0,1,0,0,0),v2=(0,0,0,0,1).

                    (c) We have W={(0,a,0,0,b):a,b∈C}.

                    6

                    Suppose v1,v2,v3,v4 is a basis of V. Prove that

                    v1+v2,v2+v3,v3+v4,v4

                    is also a basis of V.

                    Proof: For any v∈V, there is a1,a2,a3,a4∈F such that

                    v=a1v1+a2v2+a3v3+a4v4=a1(v1+v2)+(a2−a1)(v2+v3)+(a3−a2+a1)(v3−v4)+(a4−a3+a2−a1)v4

                    thus v1+v2,v2+v3,v3+v4,v4 spans V. If

                    c1(v1+v2)+c2(v2+v3)+c3(v3+v4)+c4v4=0

                    we can have

                    c1v1+(c1+c2)v2+(c2+c3)v3+(c3+c4)v4=0

                    thus

                    {c1=0c1+c2=0c2+c3=0c3+c4=0

                    which means c1=c2=c3=c4=0. Thus v1+v2,v2+v3,v3+v4,v4 is linearly independent.

                    7

                    Prove or give a counterexample: If v1,v2,v3,v4 is a basis of V and U is a subspace of V such that v1,v2∈U and v3,v4∉U, then v1,v2 is a basis of U.

                    Solution: A counterexample is p0=1,p1=z,p2=z3+z2,p3=z3, it is a basis of P3(F). let U=P2(F), then p2∉U and p3∉U, but p0,p1 is not a basis of U since p0,p1 does not span U.

                    8

                    Suppose U and W are subspaces of V such that V=U⊕W. Suppose also that u1,…,um is a basis of U and w1,…,wn is a basis of W. Prove that

                    u1,…,um,w1,…,wn

                    is a basis of V.

                    Proof: For every v∈V, we can find u∈U and w∈W such that v=u+w, then there are scalars a1,…,am and b1,…,bn such that

                    u=a1u1+⋯+amumw=b1w1+⋯+bmwm

                    this means

                    v=a1u1+⋯+amum+b1w1+⋯+bmwm

                    thus the list u1,…,um,w1,…,wn spans V. Next suppose that

                    0=a1u1+⋯+amum+b1w1+⋯+bmwm

                    we still let u,w be defined as above, then 0=u+w means u=−w, thus u∈W, from V=U⊕W we see that u=w=0, thus all ai and all bj are 0, which shows the list u1,…,um,w1,…,wn is linearly independent in V.

                                      Suppose v1,v2,v3,v4 spans V. Prove that the list

                                      v1−v2,v2−v3,v3−v4,v4

                                      also spans V.

                                      Proof: For any v∈V, we can find a1,…,a4 such that v=a1v1+a2v2+a3v3+a4v4, this means

                                      v=a1(v1−v2)+(a2+a1)(v2−v3)+(a3+a1+a2)(v3−v4)+(a4+a3+a1+a2)v4

                                      thus the list given also spans V.

                                      Suppose v1,v2,v3,v4 is linearly independent in V. Prove that the list

                                      v1−v2,v2−v3,v3−v4,v4

                                      is also linearly independent in V.

                                      Proof: Let a(v1−v2)+b(v2−v3)+c(v3−v4)+dv4=0, from this we get

                                      av1+(b−a)v2+(c−b)v3+(d−c)v4=0

                                      As v1,v2,v3,v4 is linearly independent in V, we have

                                      a=0,b−a=0,c−b=0,d−c=0

                                      which gives a=b=c=d=0.

                                      Suppose v1,v2,…,vm is linearly independent in V and w∈V. Prove that if v1+w,…,vm+w is linearly dependent, then w∈span(v1,…,vm).

                                      Proof: There exists a1,…,am, not all 0 such that

                                      a1(v1+w)+⋯+am(vm+w)=0

                                      which means

                                      (*)a1v1+⋯+amvm+(a1+⋯+am)w=0

                                      if a1+⋯+am=0, then (∗) reduce to a1v1+⋯+amvm=0 and a1=⋯=am=0, a contradiction to our hypothesis. Thus we must have a1+⋯+am≠0, let a=−(a1+⋯+am), from (∗) again we get

                                      w=a1av1+⋯+amavm

                                      this shows that w∈span(v1,…,vm).

                                      11

                                      Suppose v1,v2,…,vm is linearly independent in V and w∈V. Show that v1,…,vm,w is linearly independent if and only if w∉span(v1,…,vm).

                                      Proof: First suppose v1,…,vm,w is linearly independent, assume w∈span(v1,…,vm), then w=a1v1+⋯+amvm, this means

                                      a1v1+⋯+amvm−w=0

                                      a contradiction to v1,…,vm,w being linearly independent, thus w∉span(v1,…,vm).

                                      Conversely, let w∉span(v1,…,vm), let a1,…,am,a be such that

                                      (*)a1v1+⋯+amvm+aw=0

                                      if a≠0, then we move aw to the right and divide −a, will get w∈span(v1,…,vm), a contradiction to our condition, thus a=0, and then (∗) reduce to a1v1+⋯+amvm=0, then a1=⋯=am=0 by the linearly independence of v1,v2,…,vm, and the proof is complete.

                                      14

                                      Prove that V is infinite-dimensional if and only if there is a sequence v1,v2,… of vectors in V such that v1,…,vm is linearly independent for every positive integer m.

                                      Proof: If there is a sequence v1,v2,… of vectors in V such that v1,…,vm is linearly independent for every positive integer m, assume that V is finite-dimensional, then some list of vectors spans V, let its length be n, but v1,…,vn+1 is linearly independent, contradicts 2.23. Thus V must be infinite-dimensional.

                                      Conversely, if V is infinite-dimensional, we choose some v1≠0, this v1 must exist, otherwise V={0} which is spanned by the zero vector, thus finite-dimensional. Notice that v1 is linearly independent. We process as follows: for n≥2, suppose we have chosen v1,…,vn−1 which is linearly independent, we can always find some vn∈V such that vn∉span (v1,…,vn−1), if no such vn exist, then v1,…,vn−1 spans V, contradicting V being infinite-dimensional. Continue this process, we get the sequence v1,v2,… which we need.
